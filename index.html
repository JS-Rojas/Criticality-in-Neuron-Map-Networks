04 · Mathematical core

One coupling rule, collective complexity.

xt+1(i) = (1−ε) f(xt(i), yt(i), k(i)) + ε ht, yt+1(i) = g(xt(i), yt(i)), ht = 1/N ∑j=1N f(xt(j), yt(j), k(j)).

Quenched heterogeneity enters only through each neuron's stimulus parameter k(i), while the mean field ht couples the population. This remarkably simple mechanism extends critical-like dynamics across a finite parameter region, suggesting a robust route to intermittent and scale-free activity in brain networks without precise fine-tuning.

Main result

A minimal form of neuronal diversity can extend criticality—with potential implications for robust, scale-free brain dynamics.
